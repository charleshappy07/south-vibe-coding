1. Identification & Safety

  • All members must wear their valid South Plantation student ID visibly during club meetings, activities, and events on campus—this ensures security and aligns with school policy. southplantation.browardschools.com
  • Misplaced or defaced IDs must be replaced following school policy; avoidance may lead to accountability under the Student Behavior Plan. southplantation.browardschools.com

2. Check-In & Campus Access

  • During off-schedule or after school gatherings, members arriving as visitors (e.g., for open labs or volunteer demonstrations) must check in through the Student Affairs office and present acceptable photo ID—such as a driver's license, state ID, or passport. southplantation.browardschools.com
  • Participants must remain in designated meeting areas unless granted permission to move elsewhere on campus.

3. Parking & Pickup/Drop Off (if applicable)

  • Members driving to club events must park only in student-designated areas with valid decals. Avoid visitor, faculty, staff, or bus loop spaces. southplantation.browardschools.com
  • Students driving themselves must follow student parking rules and ensure their car is registered and insured as required by Florida law. southplantation.browardschools.com
  • Participants arriving with parents/guardians must use the car loop on Rock Island Road for pick-up/drop-off; the Sample Road entrance is reserved for buses and staff/visitor parking. southplantation.browardschools.com

4. Respect, Responsibility & Conduct

  • Members should treat peers, mentors, and school property with respect. Collaboration and constructive interaction are key.
  • Disruptive behavior—including defacing property, IDs, or equipment—may result in consequences under the school's behavior policy. southplantation.browardschools.com
  • Adherence to South Plantation's Positive Behavior Plan is expected. southplantation.browardschools.com

5. Technology & Lab Etiquette

  • Treat all hardware, software, and lab space responsibly, ensuring tools are used appropriately and maintained properly.
  • Food and drinks should be consumed responsibly, preferably outside the tech space, to avoid damage to equipment.

6. Ethical Use of AI

We encourage innovation—especially through AI—but expect it to be used responsibly. Members must:

  • Cite AI assistance: If AI (like ChatGPT or coding assistants) helps generate code, documentation, or ideas, clearly acknowledge its use in comments, documentation, or project notes.
  • Verify outputs: AI can produce incorrect or biased outputs. Always test AI-generated code thoroughly and review logic, performance, and fairness.
  • Protect privacy: Avoid feeding sensitive or personally identifiable information (yours or others') into AI tools unless explicitly permitted by policy.
  • Uphold academic integrity: While AI can inspire solutions, original work is required. Do not submit AI-generated code as your own without proper editing and understanding.
  • Ensure transparency in projects: Whether for contests, presentations, or demos, clearly disclose your use of AI tools and how they contributed. This fosters trust and learning.
